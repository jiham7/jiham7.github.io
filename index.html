Understanding the Best Posts in Reddit
Project by Christie Jeung, Jiham Lee, Larry Wang
For Doug Downey's EECS 349: Machine Learning at Northwestern University

Summary

In this project, we wanted to determine what factors played a role in determining what makes a great Reddit post.

Motivation

With around 234 million unique users, Reddit is one of the biggest and most used websites in the world. It beats eBay, Pinterest, and Netflix in usage with 8 billion page views a month. In the Reddit community, particular posts gather enough traction to make it to the front page to garner the attention of millions. We wished to create a model that consumes a Reddit post and predicts whether it will be successful. This inquiry is valuable in that optimizing the engagement levels of content posted on Reddit or other popular social media platforms is highly beneficial to content creators. Stakeholders range from the advertising industry and corporate commercial interests to individuals interacting with communities defined by subreddits.

Methodology

We gathered data from 6000 different posts from 6 different subreddits to for training and testing. Weka was used as our approach to model our data using the algorithms: ZeroR, J48, MLP, BayesNet, Nearest Neighbor, IBK, LMT, and RepTree. We held out 1000 posts to test on. The features we used are time of day posted, day of the week posted, number of comments, gilded or not, and post score.

Results

We found that our solutions did not perform as accurately as we would have hoped, as shown in the figure. Most models did not improve significantly over ZeroR, if at all. This is because no combination of features effectively differentiated between high-scoring posts and the rest of the sample. The best and most consistent algorithm that was used to train the data sets was BayesNet, Weka’s Bayesian networks algorithm.

Conclusion

Overall, there's still a lot more work to be done to improve accuracy and find specific characteristics in what makes a good post. Through data retrieval, we could only use data collection through .json retrieval in our python script and were unable to retrieve more attributes that would have improved our accuracy due to lack of time.
