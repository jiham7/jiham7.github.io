Understanding the Best Posts in Reddit
Project by Christie Jeung, Jiham Lee, Larry Wang
For Doug Downey's EECS 349: Machine Learning at Northwestern University

Summary

In this project, we wanted to determine what factors played a role in determining what makes a great Reddit post.

Motivation

With around 234 million unique users, Reddit is one of the biggest and most used websites in the world. It beats eBay, Pinterest, and Netflix in usage with 8 billion page views a month. In the Reddit community, particular posts gather enough traction to make it to the front page to garner the attention of millions. We wished to create a model that consumes a Reddit post and predicts whether it will be successful. This inquiry is valuable in that optimizing the engagement levels of content posted on Reddit or other popular social media platforms is highly beneficial to content creators. Stakeholders range from the advertising industry and corporate commercial interests to individuals interacting with communities defined by subreddits.

Methodology

Our features included the title, if the article has a flair, the date and time, how many comments it has, and the number of gold it has. We went to five different subreddits (funny, worldnews, gaming, movies, sports) and used 1000 top posts for each due to reddit's 1000 limit in each function call. Then we take the json file of the first 1000 posts and categorize each featre. Then we would classify the posts based off this good / bad score categorization. The csv output would say the time of day, weekday, number of comments, how much gold the post has, and whether the post is considered good or bad depending on the score. There were four different approaches we took to give classification to our data. For our first set of data, we trained on the top 1000 posts from the gaming subreddit using the classification threshold as one standard deviation of the mean score. The second set of data we used 5000 posts from 5 subreddits using the same classification threshold.The third set of data we used the same 5000 posts but using mean as the classification threshold. The fourth set of data we used was just 1000 posts from gaming subreddit but just using mean as the classification threshold. We held out the top 1000 posts from the relationships subreddit to test on. We used Weka to train and test our data using the algorithms shown in the figure.

Figure 1:Accuracy Results from WEKA

Results

Our models produced used different algorithms through Weka to classify our test data, and did not have much improvement over ZeroR. The most consistent improvement in accuracy over ZeroR was through the use of BayeNet algorithm. Through the slight improvement in accuracy using the BayesNet model, we found that the features most important were time of day posted and number of comments the article received.

Conclusion and Report

Overall, there's still a lot more work to be done to improve accuracy and find specific characteristics in what makes a good post. Through data retrieval, we could only use data collection through .json retrieval in our python script and were unable to retrieve more attributes that would have improved our accuracy due to lack of time.
